Why it matters

Pre-approval isn't optional
in today's Saskatoon market.

Saskatoon's market moves fast. Well-priced homes attract multiple offers and sellers won't wait for you to figure out financing. Pre-approval means you already know your number — so when the right property comes up, you move with confidence, not guesswork.

It also protects you. The federal stress test means you may qualify for less than you think. Better to know your real ceiling before you start shopping than to fall for a home you can't actually buy.

01

02

You can go directly to your bank or use a mortgage broker who shops multiple lenders. Brokers often find better rates and have access to lenders your bank doesn't. Krishna can refer you to trusted brokers he's worked with across Saskatoon.

03

Submit your documents

You'll need recent pay stubs or Notices of Assessment (NOA), proof of your down payment source, a credit check, and government-issued ID. Self-employed buyers typically need two years of tax returns and financial statements.

04

Pass the stress test

Canada's federal stress test requires you to qualify at the higher of your contract rate + 2%, or 5.25%. This ensures you can handle future rate increases. Your lender will apply this automatically — just be ready for your approved amount to be somewhat lower than you expect.

05

Receive your pre-approval letter

Usually 1–3 business days after submitting. You'll get a maximum purchase price and a rate hold — typically 90–120 days. Now you're ready to start making offers. Krishna will alert you the moment a home fitting your criteria hits the Saskatoon market.

Common questions

What buyers in Saskatoon
always ask.

What is mortgage pre-approval?+

Pre-approval is a lender's written confirmation of how much they'll lend you, at what rate, for how long. It's based on your income, debts, credit, and down payment. It's not a final commitment — but it gives you a firm budget and tells sellers you're a serious buyer.

How does the mortgage stress test work?+

Canada's federal stress test requires you to qualify at the higher of your contract rate plus 2%, or 5.25% — whichever is greater. This means you need to show you could still make payments if rates rose significantly. Most buyers qualify for somewhat less than they expect, which is exactly why getting pre-approved first is essential.

How much do I need for a down payment in Saskatoon?+

The minimum in Canada is 5% on the first $500,000 and 10% on any amount above that. On a typical Saskatoon home around $430,000, that's roughly $21,500. With less than 20% down you'll also pay CMHC mortgage default insurance, which is added to your loan amount — not paid upfront.

What first-time buyer programs are available in Saskatchewan?+

Several. The First Home Savings Account (FHSA) lets you save up to $40,000 tax-free toward your first home. The RRSP Home Buyers' Plan lets you withdraw up to $60,000. Both federal ($1,500) and Saskatchewan ($1,575) first-time homebuyer tax credits reduce what you owe. First-time buyers with insured mortgages can also use a 30-year amortization to lower monthly payments.

How long does pre-approval take?+

Usually 1–3 business days after you've submitted all your documents. The rate hold that comes with it is typically good for 90–120 days — enough time to shop and make an offer without rushing.

Pre-approval vs pre-qualification — what's the difference?+

Pre-qualification is a rough estimate based on what you tell the lender — no documents, no credit check, no real commitment. Pre-approval involves verified documents and a credit check. In a competitive Saskatoon market, sellers expect to see pre-approval, not pre-qualification.
